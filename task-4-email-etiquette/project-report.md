One situation where poor digital communication can cause problems is when a student sends an unclear email to a professor about an assignment deadline. For example, if 
the student writes a vague message without a proper subject, greeting, or clear explanation, the professor may misunderstand the request or ignore the email 
altogether. This can lead to missed deadlines, lower grades, or confusion.

This issue could have been avoided by following proper email etiquette. The student should include a clear subject line, use a respectful greeting, and clearly explain
the purpose of the email. Providing necessary details, such as the assignment name and reason for the request, would make the message more effective. Being polite and 
concise also helps ensure better communication and a timely response.
